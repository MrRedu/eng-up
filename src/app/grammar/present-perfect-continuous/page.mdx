# Present Perfect Continuous

## For vs. Since

<List>
  <ListItem className='flex items-start gap-2'>
    <Popover>
      <PopoverTrigger asChild>
        <strong className='cursor-pointer underline underline-offset-4 decoration-dotted decoration-primary/30'>For:</strong>
      </PopoverTrigger>
      <PopoverContent align="start" className="w-fit max-w-70 p-3 text-sm italic">
        Por / Durante.
      </PopoverContent>
    </Popover>
    <Typography className="mt-0!">Se usa para un periodo de tiempo (*for two hours, for five days*).</Typography>
  </ListItem>
  <ListItem className='flex items-start gap-2'>
    <Popover>
      <PopoverTrigger asChild>
        <strong className='cursor-pointer underline underline-offset-4 decoration-dotted decoration-primary/30'>Since:</strong>
      </PopoverTrigger>
      <PopoverContent align="start" className="w-fit max-w-70 p-3 text-sm italic">
        Desde.
      </PopoverContent>
    </Popover>
    <Typography className="mt-0!">Se usa para un punto específico en el tiempo (*since 9:00, since Monday, since 2010*).</Typography>
  </ListItem>
</List>

<Alert variant="info" title="Información">
  A diferencia del <Link href="/grammar/present-perfect" className="underline">Present Perfect</Link>, aquí el énfasis está en la **duración** y la **continuidad** de la acción.
</Alert>
